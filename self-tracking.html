Time Tracking

For three years I logged what I was doing every hour of every day, and how I felt at the end of each day. I was in the habit of yearly reflections, but after each of those years I actually had empirical data: how I spent my time, how I felt after spending time doing something, which things I tended to deprioritize when I had more work or got into a relationship, and so much more.

While many of the findings were probably uninteresting to a non-me person, I (unsurprisingly) found that I was significantly happier on days where I spent more time with friends, a turned which was proportional with amount that day. Something slightly different was true with family, I was much happier on days with 1-2 family hours (likely catching up over the phone), but there was no more additional gain in happiness past that (likely days I visited home (sometimes family can just tire you out)).

Days where I spent a large amount of time on my phone watching YouTube or scrolling Instagram were more unhappy, this lead me to decrease total phone time by nearly 200 hours between the first and second years and another 50 after that. A large part of this was my quitting reddit cold turkey. This was mostly replaced with increased reading (+252 hrs first-last years) and sleeping (+212 hrs). Other interesting changes include increased time with friends (+362 hrs), increased exercise (+82 hrs), increased time spent on dates / with girlfriend (+ 137hrs, although not entirely in my control), more time to meditate (+52 hrs) and more time playing board games / DnD with friends (+1 hr). These were mostly possible due to decreased video game time (-124 hrs), decreased TV/movie watching (-177 hr), and more efficient studying decreasing the time I spend it and homework (-216 hrs).

This was a project I was unsure of at first, but I'm immeasurably better off for it.

Sunrise Curtain Opener

Humans are not meant to go from REM sleep to full alert but deal with it, body. It's a response for emergency situations (the lion is already eating your brother) not approved for daily use. Unfortunately for my brain, I don't have good enough sleep hygiene to naturally wake up at around the same time. Luckily, we don't need to do this by ourselves, and out bodies naturally take cues from the environment. So, instead of just popping a ton of overpowered LEDs in my room (I did strongly consider it) I repurposed a printer my work was throwing away to automatically open my curtains incrementally over a configurable window of time before my alarm, simulating a sunrise and preparing me to wake up.
